Chlorophyll a and chlorophyll b are the primary pigments that absorb light for photosynthesis in green plants. Chlorophyll a absorbs mainly blue-violet and red wavelengths, while chlorophyll b broadens the spectrum of light that can be captured by funneling energy toward the reaction centers. Accessory pigments such as carotenoids help protect the photosynthetic machinery and absorb additional wavelengths.
